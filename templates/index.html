How Color Blinds See the World

Types of Color Blindness

  • Protanopia: absense of RED cones; difficult to distinguish shades of RED, YELLOW, and GREEN
  • Deuteranopia: absense of GREEN cones; difficult to distinguish shades of RED, YELLOW, and GREEN
  • Tritanopia: absense of BLUE cones; difficult to distingish shades of BLUE and YELLOW

What does Color of Ours do exactly?

Color of Ours aims to raise awareness of color blindness, allowing people to see under the lenses who have limited color spectrums. Not only does this experience present difficulties of being a color blind, but it also allows one to step out of the conventional way of viewing the world.

Do skies always have to be blue? Is the sun always yellow? Exploring artworks in numbers of unusual perspectives will provide an opportunity to redefine standards of color in our world.
